# Positioning Statement

## Working statement

For [target customer]
who [has this need / faces this situation],
[Company] is the [category]
that [delivers this outcome]
because [of this unique capability / approach].

Unlike [alternatives / status quo],
we [do this differently / better / in a way that matters to them].

---

## Components

**Target customer:** [Role, context, and what defines them -- not demographics, behavior and priorities]

**Need / situation:** [The specific thing they're trying to accomplish or the friction they're experiencing]

**Category:** [How the company is classified -- be specific; vague categories produce vague positioning]

**Outcome delivered:** [What the buyer gets -- not features, results]

**Unique capability:** [What the company has or does that makes the outcome possible in a way competitors can't match]

**Alternatives:** [What the buyer is actually choosing between -- competitors, status quo, DIY, or "do nothing"]

**Differentiation:** [The specific mechanism of difference -- what the company does that the alternatives don't, or won't]

## What this is not

*Common positioning statement failure modes -- confirm this statement avoids them.*

- [ ] Not a product description (doesn't just say what features exist)
- [ ] Not a brand promise (doesn't say "we're passionate about" or "we care about")
- [ ] Not category jargon ("disrupting", "revolutionizing")
- [ ] Names a specific buyer, not a company type or demographic
- [ ] Differentiation is specific and traceable to a real capability
- [ ] A competitor could not honestly say the same thing
