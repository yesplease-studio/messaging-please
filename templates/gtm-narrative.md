## Sibling handoffs

### sales-please

**What to bring:** The per-audience core claims, proof points, and objection responses from message-architecture.md, plus the sales narrative arc above.

**What to do there:** Run the outbound sequence and discovery script skills. The message architecture provides the substance; sales-please provides the qualification framework.

**Why this matters:** Message architecture tells you what to say. Sales qualification tells you who the deal is for and whether it's worth pursuing. These work together -- a strong story told to the wrong deal is still a lost deal.

### prd-please

**What to bring:** The positioning statement and the product narrative section above.

**What to do there:** These feed directly into the positioning and messaging sections of the Strategic PRD.

**Why this matters:** The product spec should be grounded in the same claim the sales team is making. If they diverge, the product and the pitch are building toward different things.

### voice-please

**What to bring:** The register notes from each audience entry in message-architecture.md.

**What to do there:** These feed the register map and audience language bank. Run voice-discover or voice-author with these as input.

**Why this matters:** The message architecture tells the voice system what to say and to whom. The voice system tells the team how to say it. Without both, content either sounds wrong or says the wrong thing.
